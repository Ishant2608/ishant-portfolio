Who is Ishant?

A Full Stack Developer from India who builds things businesses actually use — and occasionally writes about why they broke at 2am.

01My Story

I didn't start out wanting to be a developer. I started out wanting to know how the websites I used every day actually worked underneath.

That curiosity turned into a Bachelor's in Computer Applications at Kurukshetra University, and later a Master's in Computer Applications at Chandigarh University — the formal side of a habit I'd already built by then: taking things apart to understand them, then putting them back together better.

My first real development work was in WordPress — building and fixing business websites for clients who needed something that worked, not something impressive. That constraint taught me more about practical engineering than any course did: ship fast, keep it maintainable, make sure the client's customers can actually check out.

From there I moved into full stack work — React, TypeScript, REST APIs — building dashboards and business logic for a live multi-vendor e-commerce platform. The throughline across all of it has stayed the same: I care less about which framework is trendy and more about whether the thing I build actually solves the problem it was built for.

Today I split my time between full-time product engineering and freelance client work. Four years in, 100+ websites later, that's still the question I ask before writing a single line of code: what does this need to do, and for whom?

02Why Development

Three things that keep me building.

01

Tangible feedback

Code either works or it doesn't. I like that honesty — there's no ambiguity about whether you solved the problem.

02

Business impact

A website I build can directly change whether a small business gets a customer this week. That's a short, satisfying feedback loop.

03

Constant learning

The stack I started on isn't the stack I use now, and it won't be the stack I use in two years. I'd get bored otherwise.
